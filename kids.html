Interested in animals?

Here are some ways you can help them from home!

Conservation efforts

To help preserve your local animals: Learn about endangered species in your area. Teach your friends and family about the wonderful wildlife, birds, fish and plants that live near you. The first step to protecting endangered species is learning about how interesting and important they are.

What you can do at home!

Create a backyard wildlife habitat. Put bird feeders and other wildlife attractants, such as bird houses and baths.

Come to Wilson Zoo workshops!

At our zoo we hold a variety of different events to help inspire the next generation of zoo keepers, if you or your child would like to get involved, please feel free to inquire.

Interested in animals?

Here are some ways you can help them from home!

backyard conservatory

Setting up a backyard conservatory can help promote wild animals and bugs to come and create compost in your garden creating growth for your various plants.

Reduce water waste

Reducing water waste is a massive part of helping wild animals, less water waste means more potential water that can be given to animals so they dont suffer from heat exhaustion.

Not Littering

Litter can get stuck around animals necks and harm the environment please try not to litter

Organising a cleanup

Go to a local cleanup event and help your community keep trash out of our environment!
